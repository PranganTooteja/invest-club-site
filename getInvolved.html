Intro to Wallstreet

The Berkeley Investment Group offers a semester-long internship as a means of training exceptional students to become officers.The program is very fast-paced, with different topics within finance being introduced every single week. Applications go out at the beginning of each semester. Applicants are required to write up a stock pitch with heavy emphasis given on both the creativity of their ideas and validity of their variant perception, or how the analysis differs from what the Street is thinking. Upon passing the initial screening, the candidates are then invited to present their pitch to a panel comprising of current BIG officers. The 12-15 interns finally selected are expected to attend a weekend class taught by BIG's dedicated intern directors, participate in the general-member meetings, and complete weekly deliverables. Interns are evaluated on the quality of their work on personal assignments, team projects, and a final stock pitch that applies learned concepts. At the semester's end, around half of the interns are invited to come back and serve as analysts for the club fund.

Quick Facts

- We offer a highly-accelerated learning experience

- We teach advanced financial modeling

- Each semester, interns participate in a consulting competition

- Each intern receives guidance from a BIG mentor

- The average intern spends 8-10 hrs a week

- 100% of officers were interns

- We typically take 10% of applicants
